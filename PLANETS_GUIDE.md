# Complete Solar System - Quick Reference

## All 9 Celestial Bodies

### **Sun** (Star)
- Mass: 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg
- Color: Golden yellow with bright glow
- Position: Center of the solar system

### **Mercury**
- Mass: 3.285 × 10²³ kg
- Color: Gray (rocky surface)
- Orbital period: ~88 Earth days

### **Venus**
- Mass: 4.867 × 10²⁴ kg
- Color: Bright yellowish (thick atmosphere)
- Orbital period: ~225 Earth days

### **Earth**
- Mass: 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg
- Color: Beautiful blue (oceans)
- Orbital period: 365 Earth days

### **Mars**
- Mass: 6.39 × 10²³ kg
- Color: Red (iron oxide)
- Orbital period: ~687 Earth days

### **Jupiter**
- Mass: 1.898 × 10²⁷ kg (largest planet)

## Spacetime Curvature

### How It Works
The grid visualizes Einstein's general relativity:
- **Flat areas**: No mass nearby
- **Curved wells**: Mass warps spacetime
- **Depth**: Proportional to mass

### Mass Effects
- **Sun**: Creates the deepest well (most massive)
- **Jupiter**: Second deepest (largest planet)
- **Saturn**: Third deepest
- **Smaller planets**: Shallow curves

### Grid Specifications
- **Size**: 400 × 400 units
- **Resolution**: 70 × 70 grid lines
- **Max Depth**: -50 units (for massive objects)
- **Color Gradient**: Blue (shallow) → Purple (deep)

## Physics Accuracy

### Gravitational Forces
- Uses Newton's law: `F = G × m₁ × m₂ / r²`
- All bodies attract each other (N-body simulation)
- Real masses and distances (scaled for visualization)

### Orbital Mechanics
- **Verlet integration** for stability
- **Real velocities** for circular orbits
- **Conservation of energy** (approximately)

### Time Scale
- **2 days per frame** (86400 × 2 seconds)
- **60 FPS** rendering
- Watch planets orbit in real-time!

## Tips for Best Experience

### Viewing Planets
1. **Start with Perspective** (press 1)
2. **Zoom in** with mouse wheel to see inner planets
3. **Zoom out** to see outer planets (Uranus, Neptune)
4. **Drag to rotate** for different angles

### Understanding Orbits
1. **Press 2** for top-down view
2. **Enable trails** (press T if off)
3. **Watch the patterns** - inner planets orbit faster
4. **Pause** (SPACE) to examine positions

### Spacetime Curvature
1. **Press 2** for top-down view
2. **Enable grid** (press G if off)
3. **See the Sun's massive well** at the center
4. **Watch Jupiter's well** move as it orbits

### Finding Specific Planets
- **Inner planets** (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars): Zoom in, close to sun
- **Gas giants** (Jupiter, Saturn): Medium distance, large sizes
- **Ice giants** (Uranus, Neptune): Far out, need to zoom out
